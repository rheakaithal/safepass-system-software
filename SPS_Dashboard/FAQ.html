Frequently Asked Questions

How often is the data updated?

The dashboard updates in real-time, with new sensor readings being fetched every second. This ensures you always have the most current flood monitoring data.

What do the warning states mean?

State 0 (Green): Normal conditions - water level below the warning threshold
State 1 (Yellow): Warning level - water level between the thresholds
State 2 (Red): Critical level - water level above the critical threshold

How accurate are the sensor readings?

What should I do if I see a critical warning?

If you see a critical (red) warning, follow your organization's emergency flood response procedures immediately. Contact emergency services if necessary and evacuate low-lying areas.

Can I access historical data?

Yes! Use the duration selector in the graph card to view data from the past 12 hours up to 1 week. Historical data can help identify patterns and trends.

What does the "Time to Flood" estimate mean?

This is a predictive estimate based on the current rate of water level change. It indicates how much time you have before flooding conditions are expected to occur if the current trend continues.

How do I request new images?

Click the "Request Images" button in the Live View card. The system will capture fresh images from all monitoring cameras and display them within a few seconds.

Who should I contact for technical support?

For technical issues with the dashboard or sensors, contact your system administrator or IT support team. For emergency flood response, contact local emergency services.
